## Key Improvements

### 1. ✅ **Protein Requirements - Significantly Increased**

#### Before:

- Weight Loss: 35% protein, minimum 1.6g/kg
- Muscle Gain: 30% protein
- Maintenance: 30% protein

#### After (2024 Research-Based):

- **Weight Loss: 45% protein, target 2.2g/kg** ← CRITICAL for muscle preservation
- **Muscle Gain: 30% protein, target 1.8g/kg**
- **Maintenance: 35% protein, target 1.8g/kg**

#### Why This Matters:

2024 systematic reviews show that protein intake **exceeding 1.3g/kg/day prevents muscle loss** during weight loss, while **2.0-2.4g/kg is optimal** for maximum muscle preservation in calorie deficit.

**Example Impact:**

- **70kg woman on weight loss:**
  - Before: 105g protein/day (1.5g/kg)
  - After: 154g protein/day (2.2g/kg)
  - **+49g protein = significantly better muscle preservation**

---

### 2. ✅ **Gender-Specific Calorie Minimums**

#### Before:

- Everyone: 1200 cal minimum (unsafe for men)

#### After (NIH/Mayo Clinic Guidelines):

- **Women: 1200-1500 cal minimum**
- **Men: 1500-1800 cal minimum**

#### Why This Matters:

Men require higher baseline calories due to larger body mass and metabolic needs. Setting minimums too low causes:

- Metabolic adaptation
- Muscle loss
- Nutrient deficiencies
- Unsustainable diet adherence

**Example Impact:**

- **Male client with TDEE 2800:**
  - Before: Could be prescribed 1200 cal (too aggressive, unsustainable)
  - After: Minimum 1800 cal (safer, more sustainable)

---

### 3. ✅ **Percentage-Based Deficit Protection**

#### Before:

- Fixed 1000 cal deficit for everyone (can cause weight regain)

#### After (2024 Research):

- **Maximum 0.7% body weight loss per week**
- **Whichever is smaller: 1000 cal OR percentage-based**

#### Why This Matters:

Research shows 1000 cal deficits lead to:

- Significant weight regain
- Loss of lean muscle mass
- Metabolic slowdown

Percentage-based approach scales appropriately to body size.

**Example Impact:**

- **80kg male (TDEE 2800):**
  - Before: -1000 cal = 1800 cal/day
  - 0.7% weekly loss = 0.56kg/week = 616 cal deficit
  - After: -616 cal = 2184 cal/day
  - **More sustainable, less muscle loss**

---

### 4. ✅ **Optimized Macro Ratios**

**IMPORTANT: Carbs never exceed 40%** (modern evidence-based approach)

#### Weight Loss Ratios:

- Before: 40% carbs / 35% protein / 25% fat
- **After: 30% carbs / 45% protein / 25% fat**
- Rationale: Higher protein for muscle preservation in deficit, lower carbs for fat loss

#### Muscle Gain Ratios:

- Before: 50% carbs / 30% protein / 20% fat
- **After: 40% carbs / 30% protein / 30% fat** (modern lean bulk)
- Rationale: Max 40% carbs to minimize fat gain, higher fats for hormone production

#### Maintenance Ratios:

- Before: 45% carbs / 30% protein / 25% fat
- **After: 35% carbs / 35% protein / 30% fat**
- Rationale: Balanced macros with higher protein for body recomposition

## 5. ✅ **Fixed TDEE Activity Multipliers** (December 2024 Update)

#### Problem Discovered:

Our activity multipliers were **8-11% too high**, causing overestimation of daily calorie needs.

#### Before (TOO HIGH):

- Sedentary: 1.2 ✓
- Lightly Active: 1.375 ✓
- Moderately Active: **1.55** ❌ (8.5% too high)
- Active: **1.725** ❌ (11% too high)
- Very Active: **1.9** ❌ (10% too high)

#### After (FIXED - Calculator.net Standards):

- Sedentary: **1.2** ✓ (no change)
- Lightly Active: **1.375** ✓ (no change)
- Moderately Active: **1.465** ✓ (4-5 days/week)
- Active: **1.55** ✓ (daily exercise)
- Very Active: **1.725** ✓ (intense 6-7 days/week)

#### Impact Example:

**70kg Male, Active Lifestyle:**

- BMR: 1,785 cal/day
- Old TDEE: 1,785 × 1.725 = **3,079 cal/day** (too high!)
- New TDEE: 1,785 × 1.55 = **2,767 cal/day** (accurate)
- **Difference: 312 calories/day**

This 312 cal/day overestimation could prevent weight loss or cause unintended weight gain over time.

#### Why This Matters:

- **More accurate TDEE** = better results for clients
- **Matches industry standards** (Calculator.net, Harris-Benedict)
- **Prevents overestimation** that slows progress
